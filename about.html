My Story

Originally, I entered college as a Chinese Studies major but I soon double majored in International Relations with an emphasis in World Trade and Development and an area emphasis in East Asia. This stemmed from my interest in learning about people, cultures, history, and languages. My initial goal was to become a Chinese to English translator or interpreter to help bridge the language barrier many Chinese immigrants face in the United States.

During the pandemic, I worked at the Oakland Chinatown Chamber of Commerce as an Administrative Assistant and was tasked to support the redesign of our website, which was outdated, lacked essential information, and provided a poor user experience for our chamber members and community members. I was in charge of providing the engineer with necessary images and content, which I now know is the job of a UX Writer, and providing input on the design of the website, which I now know is called UI/UX Design. Through this experience, I developed an interest in UX Design, so during my senior year of college, I decided to triple major in Design.
